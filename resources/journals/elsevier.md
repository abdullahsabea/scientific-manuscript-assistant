# Elsevier Journal Guidelines

## AI Policy
Elsevier's policy requires that authors disclose the use of generative AI and AI-assisted technologies in the writing process.
- AI should only be used to improve readability and language.
- AI cannot be listed as an author.
- Authors are ultimately responsible for the content.

### Suggested Disclosure Template (Add to Methods or Acknowledgements)
> "During the preparation of this work the author(s) used [NAME TOOL / SERVICE] in order to [REASON]. After using this tool/service, the author(s) reviewed and edited the content as needed and take(s) full responsibility for the content of the publication."

## Formatting Preferences
- Abstract is typically limited to 250 words.
- References should follow the standard Elsevier numbered style or author-date style depending on the specific journal.
- Highlights are often mandatory (3 to 5 bullet points, maximum 85 characters including spaces per bullet).
